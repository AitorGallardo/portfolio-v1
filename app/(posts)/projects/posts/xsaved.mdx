If you've ever saved a tweet and never found it again, you already know the problem. X bookmarks are one endless list — no search, no way back to that one post you actually needed.

XSaved fixes that. Install it, open X, and your bookmarks turn into a proper library you can search, sort, and actually enjoy coming back to.

# Find anything in seconds

<Image
  src={`${process.env.NEXT_PUBLIC_BASE_PATH}/images/xsaved_search.png`}
  alt="Searching the XSaved library"
/>

Type a word, a name, a topic. XSaved digs through everything you've ever saved and brings it up instantly — no more scrolling for ten minutes hoping to recognize it.

# Organized the moment you open it

<Image
  src={`${process.env.NEXT_PUBLIC_BASE_PATH}/images/xsaved_library.png`}
  alt="The XSaved library, grouped by topic"
/>

Your saves arrange themselves into a clean, visual grid and group by topic, so related posts sit together. Filter by author, tag, or media type whenever you want to narrow things down.

# Keep your own notes

<Image
  src={`${process.env.NEXT_PUBLIC_BASE_PATH}/images/xsaved_notes.png`}
  alt="Adding a personal note to a bookmark in XSaved"
/>

Add a quick note to any bookmark — why you saved it, what to do with it later. The context stays with the post, so you never have to remember.

# It's yours, and it stays yours

Everything lives on your own device. There's no account to create and nothing handed off to a server you don't control. Fast, private, and entirely yours.

# Get it

XSaved is live on the Chrome Web Store. Add it, open your bookmarks on X, and watch your library build itself. See it in action at [xsaved.com](https://xsaved.com).
